# Rough Notes: Algebra for Beginners

## Introduction
- Acknowledge that algebra looks scary with letters (x, y) replacing numbers.
- Clarify that it's just a puzzle-solving language.

## Explaining Algebra
- It's essentially "what is the missing piece?"
- Think of it as a scale that must be balanced.
- Key concept: If you do something to one side, you must do it to the other to keep it balanced.

## Practical Example
- Problem: 5 + x = 12
- Goal: Get x alone.
- Method: Subtract 5 from both sides.
- Result: x = 7.

## Common Mistakes
- Forgetting to apply the same operation to both sides.
- Trying to rush the steps instead of writing them out.
- Confusion with negative signs when moving terms across the equals sign.

## Summary
- Algebra is a tool to solve problems, not just a bunch of abstract rules.
- Practice is the best way to get comfortable.
