Sachin Tendulkar – Biography

Early Life

-   Born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, India.
-   Father: Ramesh Tendulkar, a Marathi novelist.
-   Mother: Rajni Tendulkar, worked in the insurance industry.
-   Showed an early passion for cricket, trained under coach Ramakant
    Achrekar.
-   Nicknamed “Little Master” and later “Master Blaster.”

Cricket Debut

-   Made his international debut for India at the age of 16 in 1989
    against Pakistan.
-   Youngest Indian to debut in Test cricket at that time.

Career Highlights

-   Played for India from 1989 to 2013.
-   Only cricketer to score 100 international centuries (51 Tests, 49
    ODIs).
-   First player to score a double century in ODIs (200* vs South
    Africa, 2010).
-   Part of the Indian team that won the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup.
-   Holds the record for most runs in Test and ODI cricket:
    -   Tests: 15,921 runs.
    -   ODIs: 18,426 runs.

Style of Play

-   Known for his impeccable technique, timing, and wide range of shots.
-   Excelled against the best bowlers in the world, across formats and
    conditions.

Awards and Recognition

-   Bharat Ratna (2014) – India’s highest civilian award, first
    sportsperson to receive it.
-   Padma Vibhushan, Padma Shri, and Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna awards.
-   Numerous records and accolades as one of cricket’s greatest players.

Retirement

-   Retired from ODI cricket in December 2012.
-   Retired from all forms of international cricket in November 2013
    after his 200th Test match.
-   Post-retirement, involved in philanthropy, mentorship, and cricket
    promotion.

Legacy

-   Widely regarded as one of the greatest cricketers of all time.
-   Revered as the “God of Cricket” by fans.
-   Inspired generations of cricketers across the globe.
